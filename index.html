About

I'm a Computer Engineering student at the University of South Florida graduating in May 2026. My background sits at the intersection of hardware and software, with hands-on experience in embedded firmware, PCB design, backend/cloud systems, and digital hardware design.

I currently serve as Electronics Team Lead for the Society of Aeronautics and Rocketry (SOAR), where I lead the development of data acquisition and control systems for a student-built liquid rocket engine. My work includes custom mixed-signal PCB design, embedded C++/RTOS firmware, sensor integration, and system-level validation.

I also conduct undergraduate research in the SEES Lab, where I work on AI-assisted hardware design automation. My recent work involves building benchmark datasets for RTL generation and verification, scripting ASIC flows, and evaluating power, performance, and area trade-offs using tools such as Yosys, OpenLane, and the Sky130 PDK.

In parallel, I contributed to a cloud-based multi-agent capstone project built on Microsoft Azure, where I worked on backend services, cloud integration, and agent-based system workflows for enterprise-style business processes.

I'm especially interested in roles spanning embedded systems, backend/cloud engineering, digital design, and systems-oriented software development.

Generative AI for Hardware Design

SEES Lab | Python, Verilog, Yosys, OpenLane, Sky130

Conduct undergraduate research on AI-assisted hardware design automation in the Secure, Efficient, and Evolvable Systems (SEES) Lab at USF. My work focuses on evaluating how large language models can support RTL generation, verification, and hardware design workflows through reproducible benchmarking and automated analysis.

Rocket Engine Data Acquisition System

SOAR | Teensy 4.1, C++, RTOS, LoRa, PCB Design, Mixed-Signal Systems

Lead the electronics and embedded systems development for a student-built liquid rocket engine project through the Society of Aeronautics and Rocketry. The system supports real-time sensing, telemetry, and control for engine testing, combining embedded firmware, custom hardware, and full-system integration.

RISC-V SoC with ML Accelerator

Verilog, SystemVerilog, OpenLane, ASIC Design

Designed a single-cycle RISC-V processor and integrated a custom machine-learning accelerator for vector dot-product operations. This project strengthened my understanding of RTL design, verification, datapath/control integration, and physical design workflows.

  • Implemented datapath, control, ALU, decode, memory interface, and accelerator integration in Verilog.
  • Wrote SystemVerilog testbenches for functional verification and waveform-based debugging.
  • Ran synthesis, place-and-route, timing analysis, and generated a DRC/LVS-clean GDSII using OpenLane.
