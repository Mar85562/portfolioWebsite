About Me

Welcome to my portfolio! Here, you can get a closer look at my journey as a computer engineering student at the University of South Florida and discover the projects and passions that drive me.

I am currently in my third year and have a keen interest in Artificial Intelligence, machine learning, and hardware. With experience from a summer 2023 internship, I developed a mobile portal for construction workers using Flutter. I am also actively involved in the Society of Aeronautics and Rocketry (SOAR), where I contribute to projects involving control systems, data acquisition systems, and RF control systems to support our liquid propulsion rocket engine development efforts.

In my spare time, I enjoy deepening my understanding of Data Structures and algorithms, and I am currently exploring robotics topics such as rigid body transformations and forward kinematics. I also work as a mail and desk clerk for USF Housing & Residential Education, where I have built strong interpersonal and organizational skills while assisting fellow students.

This is a student-led liquid propulsion rocket engine designed to produce 250LB of thrust. As the EECS Lead, I manage the integration of electronic systems, including load cells for thrust measurement, pressure and temperature sensors, and RF systems for remote ignition and monitoring. This project allows me to apply my skills in control systems, embedded systems, and sensor integration in a hands-on, collaborative environment

Blog Website

This is a blog website I developed that gives users a simple and secure way to share their thoughts and engage with content online. Using Flask and RESTful APIs, I developed features like creating, editing, and deleting blog posts, all backed by a Postgres database. I also implemented user authentication with password hashing to keep user data safe. The website is deployed online, making it accessible and scalable. This project reflects my hands-on experience in web development and my focus on building practical, user-friendly applications
